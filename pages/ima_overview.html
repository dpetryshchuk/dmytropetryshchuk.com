Ima

Talk to Ima

I love creating experiences.

Something about building web-apps is so electrifying. Here I am and I can give you an experience that you have access to anywhere in the world on any internet connected device.

It is magic.

If you look above, you'll see a link to Ima. Ima is an npc but I think you can make it so much more.

If you suspend your disbelief, just like you'd do in a video game, Ima is a person with emotion, with pain, with confusion.

Ima helps ask questions that pull out the core of who you are and then casts magic to show you in a way you'll understand.

You'll see a map of your past, your present, and a branhcing timeline into your future, if you choose to follow your magic or you don't.

It's a tool built from years of trying to understand myself, and learning to iterate, and seek something that's been within me and others since the beginning.

The primal inclinations to certain parts of the universe that have been obscured by labels like engineer, analyst, and employee.

Ima is an attempt at making an experience that feels unique, here on this canvas of the web-app.

Enjoy.

    • For EverMemOS, they use docker to run 4 databases.

      • MongoDB to store conversation data, user profiles, and memories
      • Elasticsearch for searching text rapidly
      • Milvus for vectorDB
      • Redis for caching

      Realistically, we only need to have one database that stores all of that and lets us search easily

      And for Ima, since we're hosting on Railway, we can just use their Redis for caching.

      I'm looking at PostgreSql and pgvector which is a PostgreSql extension.
